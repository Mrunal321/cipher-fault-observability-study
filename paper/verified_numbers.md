| **ARX**           | 3 | **2.0558** | 1.712 | 2.238 | speckey_box, speck32_round, marx2_box                         |

**Ratio computation:**

- ARX mean = (2.238 + 1.712 + 2.217) / 3 = **2.0558**
- "Substitution-based" group definition: 4-bit S-box + Subst. round (total n = 7)
  - mean = (1.013×4 + 1.173×3) / 7 = (4.052 + 3.519) / 7 = **1.0817**
- **ARX / Substitution = 2.0558 / 1.0817 = 1.9006×** → reported as **1.90×**

**Scope note**: the ratio compares ARX vs the "substitution-based" pool
only. If we instead compare ARX vs *all other families* (n = 12, mean ≈
1.027), the ratio is 2.0558 / 1.027 = **2.00×**. The paper uses the
substitution-based pool because it is the direct architectural contrast
of interest (substitution-permutation networks vs ARX).

**The "18–62 %" range** is the span from the smallest reduction
(chain MAJ5, −18.12 %) to the largest (tree MAJ7, −61.87 %), so the
paper should say **"18–62 %"** (verified) — never "18–60 %" or
similar rounded variants.

**Single-gate row note**: AvgDP = 1.000 for a one-gate circuit by
construction — a fault at the gate's output always reaches the primary
output, since the gate output *is* the primary output. The masking
effect requires downstream structure (chain or tree).

## 5. Theoretical per-input masking probability

For odd arity n, the per-input bit-flip masking probability is

$$P_{\text{mask}}(n) = 1 - \binom{n-1}{(n-1)/2} / 2^{n-1}$$

| Arity n | $\binom{n-1}{(n-1)/2}$ | $2^{n-1}$ | $P_{\text{prop}}$ | $P_{\text{mask}}$ |
|--------:|----------------------:|----------:|------------------:|------------------:|
|       3 |                     2 |         4 |          0.5000   |          0.5000   |
|       5 |                     6 |        16 |          0.3750   |          0.6250   |
|       7 |                    20 |        64 |          0.3125   |          0.6875   |
